Published Authors

Over the years, Tree-Shrew Publishing has had the privelage of working with, and ultimately publishing the works of some of the largest names in the fiction category. Listed below are just some of the authors that have joined us, including an overview of their work and favoured genres. A detailed summary of each authors works can be found by clicking on their names.

George RR. Martin

The renowned American writer known for the hugely popular Song of Ice and Fire series. It is highly probable you will have heard of this series, following the release of the television show Game of Thrones, which follows the events of the book series created by George RR Martin. Martin's genres include science-fiction, horror and most notably fantasy. Although Martin is primariliy known for writing fantasy and horror novels, such as "Fevre Dream" and "The Song of Ice and Fire" series, some of his earlier works focussed on the science-fiction genre. These novels are set in a future history, known informally as "The Thousand Worlds" or "The Manrealm". [1]

J. K. Rowling

The british author Joanne Rowling, known more commonly by her pen names J. K. Rowling and Robert Galbraith is a best selling fantasy, thriller and crime fiction writer. Rowling's works include the famous "Harry Potter" series, the spin-off series "Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them", written under the pseudonym Newt scamander, as well as the political thriller "Casual Vacancy". Works published under the pen name Robert Galbraith include the crime thriller "The Cuckoo's Calling" and associated sequels. While Rowling was initially known for writing in the fantasy genre, particuarly for a younger audience, later in her career she branched off into the thriller and crime fiction genres, with more mature storylines. Neil Gaiman

Neil Gaiman is an award winning English author of fiction novels, comic books, short novels and graphic novels, as well as films. Gaiman's works commonly combine fantasy with the modern world, though his genres also include horror, science fiction and comedy. In fact one of Gaiman's earlier novels, "Good Omens" was a collaboration with the comedy writer Terry Pratchet, who is known for his comedic "Disk-World" series. Some of Gaiman's other notable works include "Stardust", "American Gods", and "The Ocean at the End of the Lane". The first two aforementioned novels being also adapted for the screen. [3]

Stephen King

The highly acclaimed American author Stephen King has produced over 54 novels, many of which have been adapted for the screen. King's genres include horror, science-fiction, fantasy, suspense and supernatural-fiction. King's early works include the supernatural-fiction "Carrie", "Salems Lot", and the famous horror "The Shining", which tells the story of the haunted Overlook hotel and was later adapted to the screen. Following this, King branched into the fanatsy/western genre with the "Dark Tower" series. King's roots are well entrenched in the horror genre, in fact he is known as one of the most successful horror writers of all time. [4]
